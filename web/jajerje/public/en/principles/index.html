Just as Moses did with the Ten Commandments, the leader drafted the ten fundamental ideas for living in harmony and in a perpetual state of well-being. The ten rules are based on the values of obedience and sacrifice, with strong roots in the philosophical school of Stoicism. They are presented in an ordered manner below:

1. You shall love the leader above all things

The first commandment of this creed establishes the supremacy of the leader as the object of devotion and absolute loyalty.

2. You shall not take the name of the leader in vain

The name of the leader must be invoked with solemnity and seriousness, reserved for important and relevant occasions.

3. You shall sanctify the victories of the regime

Any triumph, achievement, or progress obtained by the government or established authority must be revered and celebrated as a sacred act.

4. You shall honor the leader and his decisions

Honoring the leader means obeying his commands, following his directives, and showing unwavering loyalty to him and his regime.

5. You shall not kill, unless commanded by the leader

The act of taking a life is permitted only when ordered or authorized by the leader.

6. You shall not commit impure acts, nor make slanderous or libelous remarks about the leader

It implies a duty of respect and reverence towards the leader, as well as a responsibility not to spread false or harmful information that could undermine his position of power.

7. You shall not steal, unless commanded by the leader

In cases where the leader considers theft necessary for the benefit of the regime or to consolidate his power, such action is legitimized and becomes a command.

8. You shall not lie, unless commanded by the leader

In situations where the leader considers a lie necessary for the well-being of the regime or to protect his position and power, this action is justified and becomes a duty.

9. You shall not think outside the established, unless commanded by the leader

If the leader decides that it is necessary to explore new ideas or perspectives, such thoughts are legitimized and become a directive.

10. You shall not covet your neighbor's goods, unless required by the leader's political agenda

Exceptions are made in cases where the interests of the regime, under the leader's guidance, demand a restructuring of resources for the common good.
